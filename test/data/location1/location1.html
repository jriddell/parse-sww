273 Pinkston Watersports

Contributor Jonathan Riddell

Grade 2(3)

Length 100m

Location NS 593 666 (55.8727, -4.2493)

Introduction

Pinkston is Scotland’s first artificial white water
course, built by and for paddlers. No need to worry about rafters getting in the way of your run, the centre is a charity and is focused on introducing folks from all backgrounds to the canoeing world.

Access

Park and Play sessions run Monday evenings from April to October and most Saturday after-
noons in warmer months; check the Pinkston Facebook page to confirm. SCA Freestyle nights happen monthly on Tuesday evenings – check their Facebook group for dates. Clubs and groups can book out the centre themselves after doing a simple External Site Operator training course.

Description

After many years of hard work, driven by Andy Watt, Scotland now has an artificial white
water course right in Glasgow city centre with easy access from the M8. Water is filtered to keep it clean and the closed basin means the water temperature is typically a couple of degrees warmer than the surrounding rivers. Compared to natural rivers the course runs tight with strong eddies even on one or two pumps, which surprises many beginners. The best hole for playboating is before the first corner but make sure it’s on three pumps when trying loops if you value your head. Several waves down the course provide places to practice surfing as well as sections to practice ferry gliding and breaking in/out. As of 2017 the short course is still being worked on to create a perfect playhole.

Other important points

There are slalom gates, polo pitches, classrooms and even space for racing practice. The shallow water area is useful for coaching rolls. There is also plenty of parking onsite as well as disabled facilities.
