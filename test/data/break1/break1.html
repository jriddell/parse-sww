064 Elchaig

Contributor Richard Bannister

Grade 2/3 (4+)

Length 8km

Start NH 009 269 (57.2897, -5.3048)

Finish NN 941 304 (57.3169, -5.4217)

Introduction

This is a pleasant, mostly flat run with several steeper rapids.

Gauge

The best levels would be medium and above on the SEPA gauge at Killilan (River Ling). If you’re doing this as part of the Glomach run, it will be low and a bit of a scrape in places. It would be much better with more water, in which case don’t miss the Lower Ling while you’re here.

Access

Although a long slog up the valley from Killilan, at least the scenery is nice. If you could get permission to drop your boats off that would help – good luck with that.

Description

Start at a long, multi-channel grade 4 under the footbridge. Flat water leads to the first of several mini-gorges with some grade 2/3 water depending on levels. After a while comes a classy double drop into a deep pool. Further down come a couple of longer stepped rapids, the second of which bends left into a small narrow arch/sump across the flow. Probably best to portage this one (NN 974 275). You used to be able to drive up to this point, but no longer. After that it’s flat to the take-out. You could save time by carrying over the oxbow.

Other important points

Many years ago, you could drive up the glen to go and see the Glomach Falls. The current owners have now banned all car access. Don’t try to drive up or you might get locked in.

065 An Leth-allt

Contributor Richard Bannister

Grade 5?

Length 100m

Location NG 907 232 (57.2509, -5.4709)

On the road to Skye from Fort William, just west of Inverinate on Loch Shiel, this steep mountain river appears very promising on the map. The reality is a chossy rocky mess. However, one treat is saved till last. Just 20m above the road bridge is a 5m clean fall into a closed-in cauldron with a steep narrow rapid on exit. A near-roadside grade 5? challenge for those with a reliable boof and a good safety team. Swimming in the cauldron would be unappealing; several times I’ve looked at this and backed out. If you are still in your kit from another trip and it’s moderately wet, this could be worth a look. No known descent.
