306 Tweed

 

Contributors Keith Hall and Malcolm Ross

Grade 1(2/3)

Length Up to 100km

Many stretches of the Tweed are frequently paddled, from Manor Bridge, just above Peebles, downwards. It can be paddled in almost all conditions, although a flow of at least 1.2 on the SEPA Tweed@Norham gauge is recom-
mended. In high spate the river can rise as much as 5m. The technical grade 2 rapid at Fairnilee, near where the A708 forks off the A72 (NT 458 325; 55.584, -2.860), has long been used as a Div 4 slalom site. Selkirk Canoe Club runs a rodeo here in May each year as part of their River Tweed Canoe Festival event. The other main rapids are at Rutherford (NT 653 316; 55.5773, -2.5504) and Makerstoun (Gd 2/3) between Newton St Boswells and Kelso (NT 676 316; 55.5777, -2.5153). From Kelso to Berwick provides a full day’s trip that can be split into several shorter sections, and provides mainly grade 1 paddling with a few small rapids and weirs. For a more complete description see the Scottish Canoe Touring guidebook or look on the SCA website. The river is a popular fishing destination so expect to share your trip and follow the Paddlers’ Code.
